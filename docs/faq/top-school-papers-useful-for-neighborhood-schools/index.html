Are top school papers useful for neighborhood schools?

Yes — mainly as a way to expose your child to tougher questions. But don’t use them as your only practice set.

When they help

  • Your child is already comfortable with core topics and needs a challenge.
  • You use them for question exposure, not as a self-worth scoreboard.

When they backfire

  • If they replace basic practice entirely (gaps stay hidden).
  • If the child starts associating practice with constant failure.

How to use them properly

  • Start with timed mini-sections (10–15 mins), not full papers.
  • Review mistakes: why wrong, what concept, what pattern.
  • Mix in easier sets to maintain confidence + speed.
